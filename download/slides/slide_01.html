PART 1 · PROBLEM UNDERSTANDING

Recruiters are drowning in resumes —
and missing great candidates.

For every open role, ~200 applicants. ~75% are unqualified — but finding the qualified 25% means reading every single line.

THE PROBLEM
Volume vs. bandwidth

200 resumes per role. ~90 seconds to read each one. By resume #40, judgment blurs — and the strongest candidate might be #180.

WHO IT HURTS
In-house recruiters & hiring managers

Especially GCC talent-acquisition teams hiring at volume across multiple roles. The ones who care most about quality are also the most time-starved.

WHY IT MATTERS
Time-to-hire = revenue

Every unfilled engineering role costs ~$7K/day in lost output. Burnout is at record highs. And the best candidates get missed — not because they weren't qualified, but because no one had bandwidth to read them carefully.

WHAT SUCCESS LOOKS LIKE
Minutes, not hours

Cut first-pass screening from hours to minutes per role. Surface the top 10-15% with explainable reasoning. Free recruiters to actually talk to candidates instead of reading about them.

"I picked this problem because I've watched friends on both sides — recruiters exhausted by resume piles, and strong candidates who never got a fair first read."
