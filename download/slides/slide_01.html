PART 1 · PROBLEM UNDERSTANDING

Recruiters are drowning in resumes —
and missing great candidates.

For every open role, ~200 applicants. ~75% are unqualified — but finding the qualified 25% means reading every single line, by hand, at 90 seconds each.

THE PROBLEM

Volume vs. bandwidth

200 resumes per role × 90 seconds each = ~5 hours of pure reading. By resume #40, judgment blurs — and the strongest candidate might be #180.

WHO IT HURTS

In-house TA teams hiring at volume

Especially GCC talent-acquisition teams filling multiple roles simultaneously. The teams who care most about quality are also the most time-starved.

WHY IT MATTERS

Time-to-hire = revenue

Every unfilled engineering role costs ~$7K/day in lost output. The best candidates get missed — not because they weren't qualified, but because no one had bandwidth to read them carefully.

WHAT SUCCESS LOOKS LIKE

Minutes, not hours

Cut first-pass screening from hours to minutes per role. Surface the top 10% with explainable reasoning. Free recruiters to talk to candidates instead of reading about them.

Our bet EXPLAINABLE AI — every score comes with matched skills, missing skills, and written reasoning. No black boxes.
