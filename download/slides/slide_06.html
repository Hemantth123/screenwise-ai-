PART 5 · REFLECTION + ROADMAP

What I learned — and where I'd take this with another week.

buildAI TOOLS I USED

The stack behind the stack

Cursor + Claude for scaffolding the Next.js app and shadcn/ui components. GPT-4-class model (via z-ai-web-dev-sdk) as the fitment engine inside the prototype. I did NOT use AI to write the rubric or the bias guardrails — those came from reading about real recruiter workflows first.

boltHOW AI HELPED

10x on the boring parts

API routes, type definitions, component scaffolding — all 10x faster. It also caught edge cases (empty resumes, oversized batches). That freed my time for the parts that matter: rubric design, prompt calibration, and running a real evaluation experiment.

warning_amberBIGGEST CHALLENGE

Calibration, not capability

The first prompt gave almost everyone 75+. The fix — a calibration line — overcorrected: the eval showed 67% label agreement and 60% precision@10. The AI RANKS the right candidates at the top (ranking quality is solid) but is too conservative with Strong Fit labels. The #1 fix for v1.1 is tuning the calibration line so Strong Fit isn't too rare.

lightbulbCORE BET

Explainable AI wins

Resume screeners are a dime a dozen. The differentiator isn't another black-box ranker — it's a ranker where every score comes with matched skills, missing skills, strengths, red flags, and a written rationale. Recruiters don't need a magic number. They need a defensible recommendation.
